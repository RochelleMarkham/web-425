Toni Morrison

Toni Morrison was born Chloe Anthony Wofford on February 18, 1931 and passed away on August 5, 2019. As a young girl, she was instilled with a great appreciation for African American culture as well as songs and folktales. After graduating from Howard University with a Bachelor of Arts and Cornell University with a Master’s degree, she taught at several Universities. Morrison’s writing reflected mostly on the African American community and African American women. Her first book was published in 1970 and was called “The Bluest Eye”. In 1987, “Beloved” was published and won Morrison a Pulitzer Prize. Morrison also wrote several children’s books with her son and had several essays and speeches published. For her 2004 novel, “Remember”, she won the Coretta Scott King Award. She was also awarded the Nobel Prize for Literature in 1993 and the U.S. Presidential Medal of Freedom in 2012.
