Teresa Carreño was born in Caracas, Venezuela on December 22, 1853. She studied music throughout her early life and eventually moved to New York where she began touring the United States. At the age of 10 she performed at the white house for President Abraham Lincoln. Carreño traveled the world playing piano and performing. In her lifetime she composed roughly 75 different pieces mostly for piano, voice and orchestra. She died on June 12, 1917 at the age of 63.
